# The-Burnout-Predictor-People-Analytics-
High employee turnover costs tech companies 1.5x–2x an employee’s annual salary in lost productivity and recruitment costs. This project utilizes the IBM HR Analytics dataset to identify the psychological and structural drivers of attrition. By blending Logistic Regression with Organizational Psychology theories, this model predicts "flight risk" and provides data-backed interventions to improve retention.

Hypothesis: Employees with low "Job Involvement" scores and high "Overtime" freuencyq will show a statistically significant higher "Flight Risk," regardless of their salary level.

Framework:  applying the Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) model to see if "Resources" (like relationship with manager) can buffer "Demands" (like overtime)

 Problem Statement:

"In high-tech environments, turnover isn't just a recruitment cost; it's a loss of institutional knowledge. I developed a predictive engine that identifies 'early-warning' behavioral signals of burnout, allowing HR teams to intervene before an employee reaches the point of resignation.
